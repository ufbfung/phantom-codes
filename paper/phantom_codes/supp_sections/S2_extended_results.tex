\subsection{S2.6: Per-disease-group breakdown (CKM vs.~eCKM)}\label{s2.6-per-disease-group-breakdown-ckm-vs.-eckm}

A per-disease-group breakdown (CKM-track conditions, covering
diabetes, ASCVD, and CKD-3, versus eCKM-track conditions, covering
hypertension, dyslipidemia, prediabetes, and obesity) is omitted
from this v1 supplement. At n=125 the per-group N is approximately
60 to 65 records, which yields Wilson 95\% CI half-widths of ±10 to
±12pp on a
50\% rate. That is too wide to support meaningful per-group
deployment claims. Replication at the originally-targeted n=500
cohort (per-group N $\approx$ 240--260) would tighten CI half-widths to
\textasciitilde±5--6pp and is on the v2 roadmap; the per-prediction CSV already
carries the \texttt{gt\_group} column needed for that analysis.

\subsection{S2.7: Per-vocabulary-position breakdown}\label{s2.7-per-vocabulary-position-breakdown}

A per-vocabulary-position (frequency-tier) breakdown is similarly
omitted. The 12 unique cohort ICD-10-CM codes are heavily
concentrated in the top tier (obesity E66.9 $\approx$ 24\%, prediabetes
R73.03 $\approx$ 20\%, type-2 diabetes E11.x $\approx$ 31\%); the rarest tier has

per-tier N as low as \textasciitilde5 (rarest) to \textasciitilde30 (most-frequent), which is
exploratory rather than authoritative. A v2 cohort with broader
vocabulary representation (planned to extend beyond the 12-code
v1 sample) is the right venue for this analysis.

Two qualitative observations from the listing:

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{String-matching baselines fabricate by design.} Both
  \texttt{baseline:fuzzy} and \texttt{baseline:tfidf} repeatedly return \texttt{E66.8}
  (``Other obesity'') and similar near-miss codes that exist in
  ICD-10-CM but were not in the bundled FY2026 CMS validator's
  accepted-code set under the strict comparison logic. These are
  fabrications under the \emph{narrow} mechanical definition; whether
  they should be reclassified to a softer ``out-of-domain''
  category in v2 is a methodology choice.
\item
  \textbf{Frontier-LLM fabrications cluster on plausible but
  non-existent specificity.} Most frontier-LLM fabrications fall
  into two patterns: (a) confused chronic-kidney-disease subcoding
  (\texttt{N18.3} instead of the real \texttt{N18.30}/\texttt{N18.31}/\texttt{N18.32}); and

  \begin{enumerate}
  \def\labelenumii{(\alph{enumii})}
  \setcounter{enumii}{1}
  \tightlist
  \item
    over-specified type-2-diabetes-with-retinopathy codes
    (\texttt{E11.329}, \texttt{E11.339}, \texttt{E11.359}) that look like legitimate
    ICD-10-CM strings but do not exist in the FY2026 tabular list.
    The pattern is recognizable: the model is reaching for a more
    specific code than the input warrants and inventing a plausible
    suffix.
  \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}
