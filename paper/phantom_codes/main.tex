\begin{abstract}
\noindent\textbf{Objective.}\\
To characterize how large language models (LLMs) fail at clinical
concept normalization, and to separate fabrication of non-existent
codes from abstention. These two failure modes are typically
conflated in existing benchmarks.

\noindent\textbf{Materials and Methods.}\\
We evaluate eight frontier LLMs under three prompting modes
(zero-shot, constrained, and retrieval-augmented) against
string-matching baselines and a frozen sentence-transformer
retrieval baseline. Inputs are Synthea-generated FHIR Conditions
under four degradation modes. Each top-1 prediction is assigned
to one of six outcome buckets that distinguish fabrication (code
does not exist in CMS-published ICD-10-CM) from abstention (no
usable prediction).

\noindent\textbf{Results.}\\
On 125 unique Synthea Conditions across 4 degradation modes,
fabrication ranged from 0 to 5.6\% across flagship configurations
and from 0 to 12.8\% across sub-flagship configurations.
Constrained prompting eliminated fabrication for every Anthropic
and OpenAI model. Abstention proved more consequential for some
models. Gemini 2.5 Pro returned an empty prediction on 88\% of
zero-shot D4 inputs. Cost-per-correct varied by a factor of 270
across the matrix; GPT-4o-mini in constrained mode achieved the
lowest cost at \$0.0003 per exact match.

\noindent\textbf{Discussion.}\\
Hallucination appears more controllable than prior literature
suggested at this scale. However, the cohort size and the
abbreviation-stress mode are imperfect proxies for real-world
triggers. Larger cohorts with more realistic clinical text are
needed to establish the true effect size.

\noindent\textbf{Conclusion.}\\
Zero-shot LLM prompting is unsafe for clinical concept
normalization. Constrained or retrieval-augmented grounding is
required to eliminate fabrication. Among grounded configurations,
cost-per-correct drives deployment selection. GPT-4o-mini in
constrained mode leads at 94.2\% top-1 accuracy and \$0.0003 per
correct prediction. Claude Haiku 4.5 in constrained mode is a
higher-accuracy alternative at 96.8\% and \$0.0044 per correct.
Both dominate the largest frontier model tested (Opus 4.7 at
\$0.0133 per correct).
\end{abstract}
